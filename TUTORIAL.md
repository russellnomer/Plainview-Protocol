## Step 1: Target Your Request

The most common mistake is firing the Cannon at a central office. FOIA is decentralized.

**Research First:** Before you fire, check if the records are already in the "Reading Room" or the Plainview Archive.

**Identify the Component:** If you want FBI records on a specific local event, target the FBI Field Office in that district, not just DC Headquarters.

---

## Step 2: Load the Cannon (Drafting)

The "Cannon" uses a precision-strike template. A vague request is a "vampire's" best friend.

**No Questions:** FOIA is for records, not answers. Do not ask "Why did you do this?" Instead, ask for "All emails between Person A and Person B regarding Subject X from Jan 2026 to Present".

**The "Small Track" Strategy:** Limit your request to a specific date range or set of individuals to keep the document count under 50 pages. This forces the agency to use the Simple Processing Track, which is significantly faster.

---

## Step 3: Assert Your Status

To avoid being buried in "Shadow Fees," the Protocol automatically includes your fee status.

**Media/Educational Status:** As a Sentinel Auditor contributing to a public database, you are requesting a Fee Waiver. You must state that disclosure is in the "public interest" and not for commercial gain.

**Commitment to Pay:** Always include a "maximum fee" (e.g., $25) so they don't stop the clock just to ask you for a few cents in copying costs.

## Practice Cannon Tips

Before firing a real FOIA request, use the Practice Cannon in the Sentinel Training Ground:

1. **Too Broad:** "All emails from the agency" - This will be rejected. Narrow to specific people, dates, and subjects.
2. **Too Vague:** "Documents about corruption" - Be specific. Name the program, contract, or individual.
3. **Just Right:** "All contracts between [Agency] and [Vendor X] from January 2024 to December 2025, including amendments and payment records."

## Quick Reference

| Common Mistake | Correction |
|----------------|------------|
| Asking questions | Request records, not answers |
| No date range | Always specify start and end dates |
| Too many subjects | Focus on one issue per request |
| Wrong agency | Research which component holds records |
| No fee commitment | Include max fee (e.g., $25) |
